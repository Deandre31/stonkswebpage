INVESTING IN STOCKS

WHAT IS A STOCK?

A stock (also called share or equity) represents ownership in a company. When you purchase a stock, you are buying a small portion of the company, which makes you a shareholder. Stocks are typically issued by companies to raise capital for business operations, expansion, and other needs.

WHY ARE STOCKS IMPORTANT?

1. Capital Raising for Companies(IPOS)-When a company issues stock (through an initial public offering or IPO), it raises capital without incurring debt. This capital can be used for expanding operations, developing new products, entering new markets, or funding research and development.

2.Economic indicator-Stock market indices like the S&P 500, Dow Jones Industrial Average, and Nasdaq serve as important barometers of overall economic health

3.Wealth creation/retirement plan-Stocks allow individual investors, including everyday people, to invest in the growth of successful companies, creating opportunities for wealth accumulation. This is especially important for people looking to save for retirement.

ALTERNATIVE TYPES OF INVESTING

>QUANTITATIVE INVESTING-Involves using mathematical models and algorithms to make investment decisions based on data analysis, statistical techniques, and historical trends.Below is a picture of the "quant king" Jim simons, famous for utilizing mathematical models to achieve highest returns ever recorded in history

>CRYPTOCURRENCY-Involves investing in digital currencies like Bitcoin, Ethereum, and others

>INDEX-Involves buying index funds or ETFs that track a broad market index (such as the S&P 500). This strategy aims to mirror market performance over time, with lower fees than actively managed funds.

>REAL ESTATE-Involves purchasing physical properties (residential, commercial, or industrial) with the goal of earning rental income or selling for a profit when property values appreciate.

>PRECIOUS METALS-Investors buy precious metals as a store of value and a hedge against inflation. Physical gold, silver, and other metals can be bought in the form of bullion or coins, or via ETFs and mining stocks.
